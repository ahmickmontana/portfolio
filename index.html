About Me

I am Filipino-born, and I moved to New Zealand in 2014 with my family, and was homeschooled until 2016 when I started studying at Aidanfield Christian School for Years 8 through 10 in 2018. My Programming Timeline

2019 - 2021

My first introduction to programming was during my NCEA Level 1 Digital Technologies (Computer Science) subject in New Zealand. This was when I learned my first programming language, Python. During NCEA Level 1-3 Digital Technologies (Computer Science), my interest in programming gradually piqued.

2022

During my first year in University of Canterbury, I initially pursued a degree in Software Engineering, but eventually switched to Computer Science in 2023.

2023

During my second year in University of Canterbury, I took SENG201, which focuses on principles, processes, practices, technique and tools of software engineering. The project involved with this course was to create a sports manager app. In this course I learned how to code in Java, working in a small team as well as using version control.

Learn more about SENG201 here!

2024

During my third year at the University of Canterbury, I took SENG302, a full-year group project course where I learned how to design, build, and deliver a real-world web application. I used Java for backend development, while working with JavaScript, HTML, CSS, and SQL for the frontend and data management. The course taught me how to handle evolving requirements, collaborate effectively in a team, manage a codebase over time, and apply software engineering principles in a professional, client-driven environment. I also gained experience with Git, production deployment, DevOps, APIs, code testing, pipelines, and working within a Scrum framework.

Learn more about SENG302 here!

2025

During my fourth year at the University of Canterbury, I took INFO263, a course focusing on web design and development, where I deepened my understanding of building dynamic websites by using PHP for backend development alongside HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for the frontend. The course emphasized creating responsive, interactive user interfaces and integrating databases with SQL to manage and retrieve data efficiently, which helped me enhance both my full-stack development skills and the overall user experience of my web projects.

Learn more about INFO263 here!
